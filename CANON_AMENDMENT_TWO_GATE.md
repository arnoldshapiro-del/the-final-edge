# CANON AMENDMENT — "TWO-GATE GATEKEEPER, FLAG GRADING, PULLBACK COUNT, AND ORB MODULE"

**Adopted:** 2026-06-12
**Scope:** All five trading apps — the-final-edge-mes (MES Masterclass), the-final-edge (M2K Masterclass), the-final-edge-patterns (Pattern Playbook), unis-ta-bootcamp-day1 (Uni's TA Bootcamp), scalpers-sentinel (Scalper's Sentinel).
**Status:** LAW. This file is the durable source of truth. It supersedes any prior rule text it contradicts — including the original "VWAP + 9/20/200" Gatekeeper formulation. The Gatekeeper now has exactly TWO gates: the 200 EMA on the 2-minute chart, and session VWAP. The 9 EMA is not a rule. The 20 EMA is a grader, not a gate.

---

THE CANON RULES — every rule statement, lesson, quiz question, tooltip, TTS script, chart annotation, and visual in all five apps must agree with these EXACTLY:

RULE 1 — LOCATION GATES (the only two): For LONGS, at the CLOSE of the 2-minute entry candle, price must be above BOTH (a) the 200 EMA on the 2-minute chart AND (b) session VWAP. For SHORTS, below both. Gates are checked at the entry candle's close — NOT throughout the pullback. A pullback dipping below the fast EMAs during the flag is normal and expected, never a disqualifier by itself.

RULE 2 — THE 9 EMA IS NOT A RULE. Remove the 9 EMA from every entry requirement everywhere. It may remain drawn on charts as a visual reference only, and any surviving mention must say it is reference-only.

RULE 3 — THE 20 EMA IS A GRADER, NOT A GATE. A-GRADE flag: flag low holds at or near the 2-minute 20 EMA → full size permitted. B-GRADE: flag pokes below the 20 EMA but holds above both Location Gates → half size or pass. VOID: any 2-minute candle CLOSES below the 200 EMA or VWAP during the flag, OR the pullback retraces more than 50% of the pole → it is not a flag; no trade.

RULE 4 — FLAG QUALITY: THE 5-POINT GRADE (teaching content; fullest version in Pattern Playbook, appropriately scoped versions in the other four):
  1. STRONG POLE — 3+ candles, mostly one color, big bodies, small wicks, steep. Weak pole = no trade regardless of flag quality.
  2. SHALLOW — pullback retraces less than half the pole; the best flags give back roughly a third or less (Bulkowski: 10–34% retracements performed best). Eyeball test: the flag stays in the TOP HALF of the pole.
  3. TIGHT — small overlapping bars drifting sideways or gently counter-trend in a narrow, orderly channel; no large opposite-color bars inside the flag.
  4. SHORT — roughly 3–10 two-minute bars. A breather, not a campout.
  5. QUIET THEN LOUD — volume contracts during the flag and expands on the breakout candle.
  Teaching mnemonic to include: a tight flag is a staircase pausing on a landing; a loose flag is the staircase collapsing into a ball of yarn.
  Update "Know The Odds" content with honestly-caveated statistics: loose flags failed ~55% of the time vs ~85% success for tight flags with strong poles (Bulkowski, 1,028 trades, DAILY-chart data; intraday success runs lower across the board, but the tight-vs-loose gap persists). State plainly that flag tightness is a bigger statistical lever than any moving-average filter.

RULE 5 — PULLBACK COUNT: Flag #1 after the Location Gates turn green = full size (highest-probability trade of the leg — Raschke's "first pullback" principle). Flag #2 = permitted only if A-grade (its breakout is the leg's third push — the last reliable leg). Flag #3 = NO TRADE, ever: three pushes form a wedge, and the third flag forms exactly where reversal traders enter; teach flag #3 as a defensive warning, not an opportunity. THE COUNT RESETS when price tags VWAP or the 2-minute 200 EMA and a fresh setup then forms, or at a new session.

RULE 6 — GIVE-BACK RULE (most prominent in Scalper's Sentinel; referenced in the others): if open day P&L falls to 50% of its intraday peak, trading is finished for the day. No exceptions.

RULE 7 — ORB MODULE (a SEPARATE playbook; never blended with flag rules; rewrite ALL existing opening-range content — including anything labeled "Method 3" or any prior numbered methods — to match this exactly, preserving only what already agrees):
  - Range: 9:30–9:45 AM ET (15 minutes). Research-confirmed: shorter ranges outperformed longer ones in academic testing, 15 minutes is the day-trading standard balancing speed and accuracy, and 30-minute ranges enter later with stops that too often exceed the 32-tick budget. Do not change to 5 or 30.
  - Entry: the FIRST 2-minute candle CLOSE above the range high = long; below the range low = short.
  - Direction comes from the break itself. NO 15-minute or 5-minute trend alignment is required. This is intentional and research-based.
  - Confirmation: at entry, price must be on the matching side of VWAP (above for longs, below for shorts).
  - Skip conditions: skip if the logical stop (opposite side of the range, or the range midpoint) exceeds the 32-tick risk budget; skip if the range is so narrow it is noise.
  - Exit style pre-committed IN WRITING before 9:45 (default: the standard 2/2/2 scale-out). Never decided mid-trade.
  - Position size: smaller than flag trades.
  - Required expectation-setting content: ORB win rates run roughly 25–45% BY DESIGN; profitability comes from winners being much larger than losers; losing streaks of 3–5 are normal, not a malfunction; judge only on 20+ logged trades.

RULE 8 — SHORT SIDE STATED EXPLICITLY AND CORRECTLY everywhere: shorts require price BELOW both Location Gates, 15-minute and 5-minute downtrends, a bear flag, and a 2-minute close below the flag trendline. Mirror every long-side rule. Verify no text anywhere states the short rules backwards.

RULE 9 — TERMINOLOGY: always "the 200 EMA on the 2-minute chart" — NEVER "200-day," "200 day," or "200-day moving average." No surviving text may require price to be "above all moving averages," or above the 9/20/200 together, or treat the 9 or 20 EMA as an entry gate.
